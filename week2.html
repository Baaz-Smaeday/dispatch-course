The Bill of Lading (BOL) is the single most important document in trucking. It is three things simultaneously: a legal contract, a receipt for goods, and the trigger for payment when signed at delivery.

Every BOL must contain:
‚Ä¢ Shipper full details (name, address, phone)
‚Ä¢ Consignee full details (delivery address, contact, special instructions)
‚Ä¢ Carrier details (company, MC number, driver name, truck/trailer number)
‚Ä¢ Cargo details (commodity, number of pallets, total weight in lbs)
‚Ä¢ Shipper signature at pickup + Consignee signature at delivery (= POD)
BOL Workflow: Shipper prepares BOL ‚Üí Driver inspects cargo and notes any damage as exceptions ‚Üí Driver signs at pickup ‚Üí Consignee signs at delivery (this becomes POD) ‚Üí Signed POD submitted to broker ‚Üí Payment released. No POD = No payment. Always.
